How Our Mold Inspection Process Works Step by Step

Accurate mold inspection requires structure and attention to detail. Our step-by-step process identifies hidden moisture, evaluates air quality, and delivers documented findings that support clear, confident property decisions.

Step 1: Visual Property Assessment
We begin by walking through your entire property, noting visible signs of mold, water stains, humidity issues, and conditions that promote growth in every room and crawlspace.

Step 2: Moisture and Thermal Scanning
Using thermal imaging cameras and moisture meters, we scan walls, ceilings, and floors to detect hidden water intrusion and dampness without any destructive probing or guesswork.

Step 3: Air and Surface Sampling
We collect air samples from inside and outside your property, plus surface samples from suspect areas. These are sent to a lab for detailed analysis of spore types and concentrations.

Step 4: Detailed Findings Report
You receive a comprehensive report with lab results, moisture readings, thermal images, and clear recommendations. We explain what we found and what steps you should take next.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I request emergency inspection?
Request emergency inspection after flooding, leaks, sudden musty odors, or worsening respiratory symptoms. Quick assessment helps determine contamination levels and prevents mold from spreading undetected inside walls or ceilings.
How quickly can inspection be scheduled?
Emergency inspections are prioritized and often scheduled within 24 hours, depending on availability. Rapid response helps detect moisture problems early and reduce potential structural damage.
What tools are used during inspection?
We use moisture meters, thermal imaging devices, and air sampling equipment when necessary. These tools identify hidden dampness and measure airborne spore concentrations accurately.
Does inspection include laboratory testing?
When air or surface samples are collected, they are sent to certified laboratories for analysis. Results identify mold types and spore levels within the property.
Will inspection damage my property?
Most emergency inspections are non-invasive. We rely on detection technology rather than demolition, minimizing disruption while still providing accurate and detailed findings.
What happens after inspection is complete?
You receive a structured report outlining moisture sources, contamination levels, and recommended next steps. This may include removal, remediation, or preventive moisture control actions.
